What was the plan


The seagulls swoop around my head,
But I have long removed my dread


The cliff is sharp: shards, spikes, and shells
But unseen above me, I hear the bells


My mouth filled with grit and my mind filled with hope
My arms reach up and continue to grope


The bells are much louder and the seagulls are gone
And the strength of the end keeps pushing me on


My last step is eager and sends crumbling rock
But I’m up at the top, and I’m sweating with shock


I sit on the edge with my mind in a flurry
Soaring with hope but sinking with worry.


It was years ago, and sunny, and on this cliff, I heard the roar
I’d been chased here by some shadows that I hadn’t seen before


And my young face grinned, eyes sparkling with dare
My stance faced towards the shadows stood there.


I made lots of plans made on beautiful dreams
I knew what to do, my head full of schemes


I turned and sprung off in a big leap of faith
I knew twould be hard, but I’d fooled every wraith


But now on this gray morning, I wonder
If my plans were just a big, gaping blunder


What had I wanted? What was the life I’d leapt off for?
What was the plan? Where’s the half-finished author?


I’d put myself through the cost, but what was the object?
Did I even still want what I’d held as a prospect?
