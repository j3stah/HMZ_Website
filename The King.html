The King

The King


The scraping from a league away
makes the flagstones curl and pray
the vines all shiver
the leaves all wither
and into the earth
the bugs all slither
The rusty scrape and labored drag
is a filty pot and a metal rag


And finally with one last ring
into the valley limps the King
Golden horns drag on the ground
hence, causing the awful sound
He is a man, he is a beast
but his ribs are starved from any feast
the coin mail clinks against his bones
His greedy hunger gripes and groans
Not fat nor complacent is he
His jeweled fangs lash out in agony
