The Grand Canyon

The Grand Canyon


A mist veiled a great many things
The next day it revealed without abandon
Oh if only Iâ€™d had wings!
To glide into that canyon


Great fields of stone
Silent in their voices
In my heart they struck a tone
By their presence they made noises!


Many swaying little bushes
Like explorers climbing the sides
While the coiling river rushes
Sculpting masterpiece with its tides


The molds of castles one could view
But poured over with molten sunset
Deep red, Soft pink, Pure orange too
amber, bronze, yes, and russet


Sturdy towers of stone soaring
A myriad of ravines plunging
The strong river faintly roaring
Across the landscape, the sun was lunging


Sanded and stony layers there were
Like wrinkles on a face
Everything a silencer
An awe-inspiring Place


Maybe given time, given time it may
With different rivers, colors, rocks, weathers
The earth might look this way
